Tokenstimator

  1. Did you make up all this costs, charges and levies?

    No.
    All the information used is provided free for public use in the Kenya gazette, available online at kenyalaw.org. The charges fluctuate every month. However the gazettment of official charges and levies happens towards the end of each month so the rates used here may lag behind the actual rates by a month or so. The estimate given will therefore differ slightly from the actual charge.

    Currently using rates, charges and levies applicable for post-paid bills incurred and pre-paid units purchased in June 2014

  2. Where can I learn more about this charges?

    Samir's blog is a good place to start. Also make sure to read the KPLC user guide for prepaid meters.[PDF]

  3. My friend told me I'll get a better deal if I buy units in bundles of 50 three times to get 150 units instead of a one time purchase of 150 units. Is this true?

    Eh, No. The KPLC system keeps a record of all previous purchases. Buying 150 units at a go gives you the same number of units as buying bundles of 50 units three times. Conducting 3 transactions might even be more expensive due to the transaction costs charged for each bundle of 50 so you will might end up with even less units for a similar amount.

  4. Where can I get a cost breakdown for my actual purchase?

    5. Physical vendors e.g kplc offices, neighborhood vendors e.t.c provide a reciept with the itemised charges. Online vendors such as www.kenyapowertokens.com provide a breakdown of charges for previous purchases transacted through them. Unfortunately mobile money purchases do not provide an itemised reciept.

  5. How can I help?

    Petition KPLC to provide an official portal to query the specific units a purchase will avail for a specific meter number.
